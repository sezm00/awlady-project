Creative Arts Programs

At Awlady Orphanage, we believe in the power of creativity to inspire, heal, and empower. Through our innovative Creative Art Programs, children have the opportunity to explore their artistic talents and unleash their imagination in a supportive and nurturing environment.


Vocational Training at Zein Art School


Our Zein Art School offers a diverse range of vocational training programs designed to ignite creativity and develop practical skills. Under the guidance of experienced instructors, children have the chance to learn various art forms, including sewing, batik, jewelry-making, mosaic, carpet weaving, carpentry, and pottery. These hands-on workshops not only provide valuable vocational skills but also foster self-expression and confidence.

Unlocking Creativity and Potential


Through our Creative Art Programs, children at Awlady Orphanage have the opportunity to explore their artistic interests, express themselves freely, and develop a sense of pride in their accomplishments. Whether they're crafting intricate jewelry pieces, weaving vibrant carpets, or sculpting clay masterpieces, each child is encouraged to discover their unique talents and unleash their creative potential.

Fostering Self-Expression and Confidence


Art is a universal language that allows children to express their thoughts, feelings, and experiences in a safe and non-judgmental space. Whether through painting, sculpture, or textile design, our Creative Art Programs provide children with a platform to share their stories, dreams, and aspirations. With each brushstroke or carve of clay, they gain confidence in their abilities and learn to trust in their unique voice.

Special Thanks to Lamia Hassanein and Heba Abou Hamda


We extend our heartfelt gratitude to Lamia Hassanein and Heba Abou Hamda, the pioneers of our Zein Art School project. Their vision, dedication, and passion for art education have transformed the lives of countless children, offering them a path to creativity, self-discovery, and personal growth.
